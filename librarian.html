Librarian

With the Spoleto Festival USA orchestra management team in 2016. (L-R Assistant Orchestra Managers Katelyn Egan and Matthew McMahon, with Orchestra Manager Michelle Zwi.)

My first experience as an Orchestra Librarian came when I was 16 and home for the summer from boarding school; the librarian of my hometown orchestra, the Owensboro Symphony Orchestra, in Kentucky, regularly brought in high school musicians to teach the basics of librarianship. Five years later, during a break from school, the OSO was in need of an Assistant Orchestra Librarian, a position I took for the season while continuing to play in the cello section.

Several years later, in 2013, coincidence arrived again as the Sinfonia Gulf Coast of Destin, FL, needed a new librarian mid-season. Already serving as Associate Principal Cello, I agreed to take on the additional responsibility and served in both positions for the following two and a half years. SGC collaborates with a local ballet company to produce two fully staged ballets per year, in addition to monthly performances of classical, pops, and educational programming.

With the NYO library team in 2017. (L-R Head Librarian Fabrice Curtis, myself, Library Apprentice Katie Knox, and Artistic Operations Manager Amanda Fischer.)

Realizing how much I enjoyed library work, I applied for and was hired to be the head librarian for the Spoleto Festival USA , a position I held for four years. The Spoleto Festival is an annual arts festival in Charleston, SC, known for its adventurous programming and emphasis on new music; the festival brings extremely talented young musicians together into an ensemble that performs several large orchestral concerts, multiple operas, and numerous chamber programs within a few short weeks. Before my third season, I initiated the creation of an Assistant Librarian position, for which I screened resumes, conducted interviews, handled hiring, and oversaw the Assistant's hours and duties throughout the festival. I also performed as a member of the orchestra for Spoleto's large-scale gala concerts. Part of the 2019 gala concert, for which I prepared all music and also performed as a member of the cello section, was broadcast as part of Performance Today and can be heard here.

In 2017, I served as the Assistant Librarian for the National Youth Orchestra of the United States, a Carnegie Hall program. I assisted the head librarian with the main NYO ensemble and served as the head librarian for NYO2, the younger ensemble. I toured with NYO2 to Philadelphia's Verizon Hall, where the orchestra performed a concert in conjunction with the Philadelpha Orchestra, and with NYO to its performance on the Carnegie Hall stage. Together, the head librarian and I oversaw and mentored an apprentice librarian.

With the Aspen Philharmonic Orchestra management team in 2019. (Stage manager Jordan Mattson, center, and Orchestra Manager Cameron Reeves, right.)

In 2018, I was hired as the Orchestra Librarian for the South Bend Symphony in Indiana, again taking the position mid-season due to an unexpected situation. Although the SBSO is a part-time orchestra, it puts on an impressive 16 concerts each season. Here, too, I regularly played in the cello section while preparing the ensemble's music.

A member of the six-person library team for the Aspen Music Festival and School in 2019, I was the librarian for the Aspen Philharmonic Orchestra, served as librarian for a number of special concerts including the 4th of July band program, and assisted the head librarian whenever requested. In this role, I oversaw and helped train AMFS students who were selected to work part-time in the library.

I have been a finalist or runner-up for full-time positions with major orchestras, including the Orlando Philharmonic, the Houston Grand Opera, and the Detroit Symphony Orchestra. Recently, I have worked part-time for the libraries of the Grand Rapids Symphony and the Detroit Symphony Orchestra. I am also available to take on digital engraving commissions for those who need arrangements, transpositions, or transcriptions from hand-written or poorly engraved materials; you can contact me ✉ here.
