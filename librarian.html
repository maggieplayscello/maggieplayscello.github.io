Librarian

My first experience as an Orchestra Librarian came when I was 16 and home for the summer from boarding school; the librarian of my hometown orchestra, the Owensboro Symphony Orchestra, in Kentucky, regularly brought in high school musicians and taught them the basics of librarianship. Five years later, during a break from school, the OSO found itself in need of an Assistant Orchestra Librarian, a position I took for the season while continuing to play in the cello section. My duties were primarily marking parts and communicating with musicians, and included marking bowings, cuts, making a full new set for a production of Le Nozze di Figaro, as well as the usual maintenance of the collection and records keeping.

Several years later, in 2013, coincidence arrived again as the Sinfonia Gulf Coast of Destin, FL, needed a new librarian mid-season. Already serving as Associate Principal Cello, I served in both positions for the following two and a half years. SGC collaborates with a local ballet company to produce two fully staged ballets per year, in addition to monthly programs of classical or pops.

With the Spoleto Festival USA orchestra management team in 2016. (L-R Assistant Orchestra Managers Katelyn Egan and Matthew McMahon, with Orchestra Manager Michelle Zwi.)

Deciding that I truly enjoyed library work, I applied for and was hired to be the head librarian for the Spoleto Festival USA, a position I held for four years. The Spoleto Festival is known for its adventurous programming, and I gained significant experience with opera and contemporary repertoire as a result. Before my third season, I initiated the creation of an Assistant Librarian position, for which I screened resumes, conducted interviews, handled hiring, and oversaw the Assistant's hours and duties throughout the festival. I also performed as a member of the orchestra for Spoleto's large-scale gala concerts. Part of the 2019 gala concert, for which I prepared all music and also performed as a member of the cello section, was broadcast as part of Performance Today and can be heard here.

In 2017, I served as the Assistant Librarian for the National Youth Orchestra of the United States, a Carnegie Hall program. I assisted the head librarian with the main NYO ensemble and served as the head librarian for NYO2, the younger ensemble. I toured with NYO2 to Philadelphia's Verizon Hall, where the orchestra performed a concert in conjunction with the Philadelpha Orchestra, and with NYO to its performance on the Carnegie Hall stage.

With the NYO library team in 2017. (L-R Fabrice Curtis, myself, Katie Knox, and Amanda Fischer.)

In 2018, I was hired as the Orchestra Librarian for the South Bend Symphony in Indiana, again taking the position mid-season due to the orchestra's unexpected situation. Although the SBSO is a part-time orchestra, it puts on an impressive 16 concerts each season. Here, too, I regularly` played in the cello section while preparing the ensemble's music. During this time, I have also worked as needed for the library of the Grand Rapids Symphony.

A member of the six-person library team for the Aspen Music Festival and School in 2019, I was the head librarian for the Aspen Philharmonic Orchestra, served as librarian for a number of special concerts including the 4th of July band program, and assisted the head librarian whenever requested. In this role, I oversaw and helped train AMFS students who were selected to work part-time in the library.

I have been a finalist or runner-up for several full-time positions with major orchestras, including the Houston Grand Opera, the Orlando Philharmonic, and the Detroit Symphony Orchestra. Most recently, I have worked in a part-time capacity for the Detroit Symphony Orchestra.

With the Aspen Philharmonic Orchestra management team in 2019. (Jordan Mattson, center, and Cameron Reeves, right.)

I will always maintain a passion for orchestra library work done well. I enjoy digital engraving and, having done an enormous amount of transcription and arrangement, am very fluent at it and am available to take on projects on an individual basis.
