Black-Owned Bookstores

There are many Black-owned bookstores in the Chicago area, and you should purchase books for this club (and any others!) as often as possible to support the community. Please avoid Amazon and big-box stores as much as possible. You can also check with your local public library! If they don't own a copy, they may be willing to order one and put it on hold for you, borrow it from another library district, or they may be able to connect you to e-book or audiobook options.

Art and Other Media

As artists, we should always be striving to expand our own viewpoints beyond the Euro-centric education we grew up with and continue to see perpetuated by major institutions. Seeking out these voices is crucial to our growth.

Are We Hearing A Crescendo of Anti-Racism in Classical Music?
Getting Curious with Jonathan Van Ness

Dr. Kira Thurman and Ashleigh Gordon join Jonathan to discuss the remarkable work of Black classical composers and musicians through history and today.

Published July 8, 2020

DSO Replay
Detroit Symphony Orchestra

Currently accessible for free, the Detroit Symphony Orchestra has collected videos from their recorded broadcasts of works by Black composers. Particularly recommended is the Violin Concerto by Wynton Marsalis, which was commissioned by an international coalition of orchestras and within the five years since its premeiere, has already been performed by many major American orchestras.
