Cellist || Orchestra Librarian || Developer

Hello! I'm Maggie. I'm a professional cellist with over fifteen years of orchestral and chamber experience. I'm a librarian who has worked for public libraries and with regional orchestras and major music festivals. I'm an educator who has taught in public schools and been a teaching assistant at major institutions. And now, I'm bringing my curiosity, enthusiasm, and excitement for learning new skills to software development and website design.

As someone who's innately curious, my life has centered itself around fields where there is always something new to learn and where I can surround myself with like-minded people. I fell in love with libraries at an early age and they've been a central part of my life for as long as I can remember. Almost immediately after I began playing the cello, I realized (with the help of my local library's surprisingly vast CD collection) that the world of classical music was seemingly bottomless. As I advanced through school and embarked on a professional performing career, I was kept engaged by its vast reservoir of literature, the constantly-changing repertoire each season, and the vibrant new music scene. However, my tendency to research and analyze every piece I was learning, along with an increasing fastidiousness about the parts I was using, resulted in an inevitable pull into the world of the orchestra library.

As an orchestra librarian, I was responsible for the researching, ordering, preparing, and distributing music for ensembles and music festivals, often while still performing as a member of the orchestra. I sincerely enjoyed the editing and formatting required to make each piece of music accurate, aesthetically pleasing, and optimally functional for each musicianâ€™s needs. It enhanced my organization and communication skills as I managed complex projects, usually remotely, within strict timelines. Like performing, it allowed me to work with amazing musicians and in venues from Carnegie Hall to the mountains of Aspen, Colorado.

As an elementary school student in computer class.

I've been a lifetime patron of public libraries, but I've also worked for several. Helping patrons search the collection and teaching them how to utilize online resources was one of the most rewarding parts of the job; trading reading and media recommendations with coworkers and friendly patrons was another. The frequency with which we were called upon to help patrons how to use library-provided apps or programs made me appreciate good design in software and websites. It gave me a passion for the many ways that easily accessible and widely available knowledge, especially with the aid of technology, can improve lives in way large and small. My time working at the front desks of public libraries gave me a front-row seat to successes and failures of technology, and in many ways planted the desire that would eventually grow and lead me to attend a coding bootcamp.

When I started to investigate software development, I realized that my skills and background would translate very well into this world. As a trained classical musician, I understand the value of every detail -- both in terms of accuracy and aesthetics. I love the challenge of improving efficiency with creative solutions and customizing my output to make the jobs and lives of others easier and more productive. I'd love to find a Junior Developer position with a company where I can continue to improve my newly-acquired coding abilities while deploying skills and strengths I've honed in my other careers.
