Educator

Raised in a family of teachers, I first began teaching while still an undergraduate. Early teaching experiences included teaching private lessons through the Central Music Academy in Lexington, KY, a program which offers free lessons to young musicians based on their eligibility for free and reduced-price lunches, and as a part of the after-school programs of Montgomery County Public Schools in Mt. Sterling, KY. Later, I became a recurring substitute teacher in the Owensboro City and Daviess County Public Schools, with a three-month stint at the latter where I taught 5th through 12th grade from the beginning of the school year through their first concerts.

I am holding a 1/2-sized cello like a guitar before an instrument petting zoo at the Freeport Elementary School in Florida.

I have coached sectionals and chamber groups for the Owensboro Symphony Youth Orchestra in Kentucky, the Jacksonville Youth Orchestras in Florida, and the National Youth Orchestra in New York.

As a Teaching Assistant at the Brevard Music Festival in North Carolina, I taught supplemental lessons to all high school cellists and taught daily sectionals on their current orchestral repertoire while a student myself in the college division. At the conclusion of the program, I received the Best College Student Award, consisting of an invitation to return the following summer with a full scholarship. This award was granted by a unanimous vote by the festival's faculty.

As a Graduate Assistant at Florida State University, I not only participated in orchestral, opera, and chamber performances as assigned, but taught several undergraduate non-majors, taught undergraduate music majors when faculty was out of town, and coached chamber ensembles.

As a member of the Giddens String Quartet, based in Florida, I performed educational outreach programs across the Florida panhandle.
