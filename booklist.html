Book Club Reading List



So You Want To Talk About Race
Ijeoma Oluo

"Oluo gives us—both white people and people of color—that language to engage in clear, constructive, and confident dialogue with each other about how to deal with racial prejudices and biases."
—National Book Review.

272 pages. ISBN-10: 1580058825 ISBN-13: 978-1580058827


How to be Less Stupid About Race: On Racism, White Supremacy, and the Racial Divide
Crystal Marie Fleming

“How to Be Less Stupid About Race takes no prisoners. Whether right, left, or center, all who are complicit with white racism face a withering confrontation. For me, the spiritual heart of the book lies in the consecutive analyses of the Obama and Trump presidencies. Fleming demonstrates that deep disdain for the latter need not mean nostalgia for the former.”
—William Darity Jr., Duke University

256 pages. ISBN-10: 0807039845 ISBN-13: 978-0807039847


How to Be an Antiracist
Ibram X. Kendi

#1 New York Times Bestseller, which hailed it as “The most courageous book to date on the problem of race in the Western mind.” Also named one of the best books of the year by The New York Times Book Review, Time, NPR, and The Washington Post.

320 pages. ISBN-10: 0525509283 ISBN-13: 978-0525509288


Citizen
Claudia Rankine

Claudia Rankine's 2014 book was finalist for the National Book Award in Poetry, winner of the National Book Critics Circle Award in Poetry, winner of the NAACP Image Award, and winner of the L.A. Times Book Prize.

160 pages. ISBN-10: 1555976905 ISBN-13: 978-1555976903

Lucy Negro, Redux: The Bard, a Book, and a Ballet
Caroline Randall Williams

"Part lyrical narrative, part bluesy riff, part schoolyard chant and part holy incantation."
― New York Times
This hybrid artistic creation makes use of poetry, essays, and historical perspectives on race and desire to create a uniquely poignant work of art. This in turn inspired The Nashville Ballet to create a new work using Williams's poetry in collaboration with dance and music by other black woman artists. A Youtube video about its creation is recommended viewing, and Williams's powerful opinion piece in the New York Times will contribute to our conversation.

119 pages. ISBN-10: 0997457821 ISBN-13: 978-0997457827
