Cellist

Maggie was fortunate to grow up around music: she sang in choirs and had piano lessons from an early age, though she did not find her true voice until she began cello lessons at age 12. She quickly blossomed as a cellist, enrolling in the Interlochen Arts Academy as a sophomore and, upon graduation, began her undergraduate studies at the Eastman School of Music as a student of Alan Harris. However, like many young musicians, she incurred a repetitive motion injury which became an increasing burden on her career path. With Professor Harris's advisement, she transferred to the studio of Benjamin Karp at the University of Kentucky to be closer to home in hopes that less stress would help her heal. However, the injury worsened and midway through her undergraduate degree she took time off to have bilateral surgery for Thoracic Outlet Syndrome. During this time, she developed side careers as an orchestra librarian and private cello teacher while recuperating; as soon as she was physically able, she began freelancing with nearby orchestras and eventually re-enrolled to complete her undergraduate degree at Grand Valley State University, then her master's degree at Florida State University with Gregory Sauer.

She attended the Brevard Music Festival four times: once as a Teaching Assistant and every time on a full scholarship. She also attended the National Music Festival in Virginia.

Having completed her master's degree, Maggie has performed as a freelance musician across the country. She has been a member of professional ensembles in Michigan, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, Alabama, and Florida.
