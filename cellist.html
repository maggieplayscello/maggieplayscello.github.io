Cellist

I was fortunate to grow up around music: from an early age, I sang in choirs and had piano and violin lessons. I was even a member of the National Youth Choir and performed in Carnegie Hall for the first time at age 11. However, it was not until I began cello lessons at age 12 that music became more than a hobby. After only two years of lesson, I was accepted to the Interlochen Arts Academy and enrolled as a sophomore with a merit-based scholarship. After graduating, I began my undergraduate studies at the Eastman School of Music in Rochester, NY as a student of Alan Harris. However, like many young musicians, I incurred a repetitive motion injury which became an increasing strain n=on my development as a musician. Under Professor Harris's advisement, I transferred to the studio of Benjamin Karp at the University of Kentucky with hopes that a less stressful environment would help me heal. However, the injury worsened and midway through my undergraduate degree I took time off to have bilateral surgery for Thoracic Outlet Syndrome. While recuperating, I developed my skills as an orchestra librarian and private cello teacher; as soon as I was physically able, I resumed freelancing with nearby orchestras and eventually re-enrolled to complete my undergraduate degree at Grand Valley State University, well known for their New Music Ensemble. I then completed my master's degree with Gregory Sauer at Florida State University, where I was a Graduate Assistant.

During my undergraduate years, I attended the Brevard Music Festival four times: once as a Teaching Assistant and every time on a full scholarship. I also attended the National Music Festival in Virginia. At both festivals, I sat principal at least once each season and participated in chamber ensembles.

Playing as a member of the Spoleto Festival USA Orchestra in June 2019.

Having completed my master's degree, I have performed as a freelance musician across the country. I have been a member of professional ensembles in Michigan, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, Alabama, and Florida. I have achieved leadership roles with many including Acting Principal Cello with both the Albany Symphony Orchestra in Georgia and Sinfonia Gulf Coast in Florida. While functioning primarily as the Orchestra Librarian for the Spoleto Festival USA, I have been fortunate enough to participate in the annual gala concert as a member of the orchestra: part of the 2019 gala concert was aired on the long-running public radio program Performance Today, and can be heard here.
