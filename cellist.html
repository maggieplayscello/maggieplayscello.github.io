Cellist

I was fortunate to grow up around music: I sang in choirs and had piano lessons from an early age, though I did not find my true voice until she began cello lessons at age 12. I quickly blossomed as a cellist, enrolling in the Interlochen Arts Academy as a sophomore and, upon graduation, began my undergraduate studies at the Eastman School of Music as a student of Alan Harris. However, like many young musicians, I incurred a repetitive motion injury which became an increasing burden on my career path. With Professor Harris's advisement, I transferred to the studio of Benjamin Karp at the University of Kentucky to be closer to home in hopes that less stress would help me heal. However, the injury worsened and midway through my undergraduate degree I took time off to have bilateral surgery for Thoracic Outlet Syndrome. During this time, I developed side careers as an orchestra librarian and private cello teacher while recuperating; as soon as I was physically able, I began freelancing with nearby orchestras and eventually re-enrolled to complete my undergraduate degree at Grand Valley State University, then my master's degree at Florida State University with Gregory Sauer.

I attended the Brevard Music Festival four times: once as a Teaching Assistant and every time on a full scholarship. I also attended the National Music Festival in Virginia.

Having completed my master's degree, I have performed as a freelance musician across the country. I have been a member of professional ensembles in Michigan, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, Alabama, and Florida. I have been a member of many of these orchestras and have achieved leadership roles with many including Acting Principal Cello with the Albany Symphony Orchestra in Georgia and the Sinfonia Gulf Coast in Florida. While functioning primarily as the Orchestra Librarian for the Spoleto Festival USA, I have been fortunate enough to participate in the annual gala concert as a member of the orchestra: part of the 2019 gala concert was aired on Performance Today and can be heard here.
